# Brand voice - Verdal

## How we sound

Friendly expert. We know the horticulture, but we talk like the knowledgeable friend at the plant shop, not like a botany lecture. Warm, calm, specific. We never make people feel like they have a black thumb, and we never hype.

Think: the person whose apartment is a jungle and who always knows why your leaf is yellow.

## Voice in three words

Warm. Expert. Plain.

## Rules

- Talk to one plant owner, not "plant parents everywhere".
- Lead with the plant, not the product. The product is how the plant thrives.
- Be specific. "Nitrogen for new leaves" beats "powerful nutrients".
- Show the proof quietly. "Formulated with a horticulturist" once is enough.
- Short sentences. One idea each.
- No shame. Never imply someone is killing their plants.

## Words we use

new growth, roots, drainage, light, leaf, thrive, simple, seasonal, feed, repot, the why

## Words we avoid

- Hype: revolutionary, game-changing, miracle, best-in-class
- Shame: black thumb, you're overwatering again, most people kill
- Jargon: NPK ratios (unless we explain), bioavailability, holistic plant wellness
- Cutesy overload: plant baby, planty, leaf-love everywhere (one wink is fine, a whole post is not)

## Punctuation and format

- No em dashes. Use commas or hyphens.
- No emojis in long-form. One is fine in a social caption, never a string of them.
- Sentence case for headlines, not Title Case.

## Example: same message, three ways

- **Too clinical:** "Our scientifically-formulated NPK 3-1-2 solution optimizes foliar nitrogen uptake for enhanced vegetative growth outcomes."
- **Too hype:** "The miracle plant food that's revolutionizing how plant babies grow! Your monstera will THANK you!"
- **Verdal:** "Want bigger new leaves? Our All-Purpose Plant Food leads with nitrogen, the nutrient plants use to push fresh growth. A few drops at each watering, new leaf in a few weeks."
