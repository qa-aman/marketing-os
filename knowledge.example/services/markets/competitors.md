## Per-competitor deep dive

### Demand gen agencies (Refine Labs, Metadata, Directive, etc.)

- **What they do well**: channel execution at scale, paid media expertise, established playbooks
- **Where they're weak**: treat marketing as a channel problem, not a system problem. Don't fix ICP, messaging, or content strategy - they execute into broken foundations. High churn when paid budgets get cut.
- **How they message**: "demand creation", "dark funnel", channel-specific ROI
- **Their ideal customer**: Series C+ company with dedicated marketing team and $50K+/month paid budget
- **Our angle when competing**: "An agency will spend your budget well, but they won't fix why conversion is low. We fix the system first."
- **Our risk**: if a company just wants paid execution and has the strategy sorted, we are not the right choice
- **Recent moves**: Refine Labs expanded into content strategy; some agencies now offer embedded roles

### Fractional CMO firms (CMO.works, Chief Outsiders, Marketri)

- **What they do well**: senior strategic access at lower cost than FTE; good for board-level confidence
- **Where they're weak**: advisory only, no execution. Client team still has to produce everything. Doesn't solve the bandwidth problem.
- **How they message**: "strategic leadership without the hire risk"
- **Their ideal customer**: CEO who needs a strategic partner and has a capable team to execute
- **Our angle when competing**: "A fractional CMO tells you what to build. We build it."
- **Our risk**: if the client has a strong VP of Marketing and just needs senior counsel, fractional CMO is the right answer
- **Recent moves**: some fractional CMO firms now offer thin execution tiers; blurring the line

### In-house CMO or VP of Marketing hire

- **What they get**: full-time ownership, cultural alignment, long-term equity upside
- **What they underestimate**: 6-12 month ramp, $200K+ total comp, high cost if wrong hire
- **Our angle**: "A Clearpath engagement can de-risk the hire. We build the foundation, document the playbook, then you hire the person to run it. We've helped 8 clients do exactly this."
- **Our risk**: companies that have the budget and patience for the hire don't need us

### Boutique GTM consultancies

- **Direct competitors**: Winning by Design (more sales-focused), Revenue Acceleration (similar positioning), Kalungi (SaaS marketing focus)
- **What differentiates us**: embedded execution model, 90-day impact commitment, focus on leaving behind a system that runs independently

## Where we win

1. **Execution, not just strategy.** Every competitor in our category either does strategy only or channel execution only. We do both.
2. **90-day impact commitment.** Most firms hedge on timelines. We put pipeline metrics on the table from the start.
3. **We leave and the machine still runs.** Agencies create dependency. CMO firms create dependency. We are the only firm that measures success by whether we've made ourselves unnecessary.
4. **Stage specialization.** We only work with Series B/C. We know this stage better than any firm that works from seed to enterprise.

## Where we lose

1. **Brand work.** Clients who need a rebrand, new identity, or creative campaign go elsewhere. We are not the right firm.
2. **Paid-only mandates.** Companies who just want paid media management pick a performance agency. We only do paid as part of a broader system.
3. **Speed.** A large agency can staff 10 people on a project in a week. We are 18 people and we are selective. If a client needs to start tomorrow with 5 FTEs, we are not the answer.
