# ICP and Personas (02-05-2026)

## Primary persona: "Priya, VP Marketing at a scaling B2B SaaS"

### Demographics
- Role: VP of Marketing or Head of Marketing
- Seniority: VP / Director
- Team size: 1-3 (herself, one content or demand gen person, sometimes a designer)
- Reports to: CEO or CRO

### Firmographics
- Company stage: Series B or C
- Employees: 80-400
- Revenue: $15M-$80M ARR
- Industry: B2B SaaS (any vertical - fintech, HR tech, compliance, dev tools, ops)
- Geographies: US, Canada, UK

### Goals (what she's measured on)
1. Pipeline generated (marketing-attributed, not just influenced)
2. Inbound as a percentage of new business (target: 30-40% for most of our clients)
3. Content efficiency (output per team member, not just total volume)

### Pains (today)
1. She inherited a marketing function that did mostly brand and events - no demand gen infrastructure
2. The CEO still owns most of the pipeline through his network. Priya is under pressure to change that.
3. She's stretched too thin to do deep work. She manages up, manages the team, and tries to create content, all at once.
4. Past agency experiences were disappointing - lots of deliverables, no pipeline, no ownership of results
5. She doesn't have the budget for a full senior marketing team but needs senior-level thinking

### Jobs-to-be-done
- "When my CEO asks 'where is pipeline going to come from next quarter,' I want to point to a system that's generating it predictably, not explain why it takes 6 months."
- "When I'm evaluating consultants, I want to see that they've solved this exact problem for a company like mine, so I know they won't be learning on our budget."
- "When my team is overwhelmed, I want to cut scope without cutting results, so I need to know which 20% of activity is driving 80% of pipeline."

### How she buys
- **Discovery**: peer recommendations from other VPs of Marketing (Slack communities, LinkedIn DMs), CEO referrals, content that demonstrates the exact expertise she needs
- **Influencers**: the CEO signs off. In larger orgs, CFO reviews spend over $15K/month.
- **Decision criteria** (ranked):
  1. Evidence of results with companies like hers (stage, industry, team size)
  2. Speed to first impact (she cannot wait 6 months for results)
  3. Team quality (who will actually work on her account, not just who pitches)
  4. Flexibility to adjust scope
  5. Cultural fit (she wants peers, not vendors)
- **Buying committee**: Priya proposes, CEO approves. Sometimes CRO is consulted if the engagement touches pipeline metrics.
- **Sales cycle**: 30-60 days. Longer if procurement is involved (rare at this stage).
- **Common objections**: "We tried an agency before and it didn't work", "I'm not sure we're ready for this level of investment", "Can we start smaller?"

### Where she hangs out
- **Communities**: Demand Gen Visionaries Slack, Pavilion, MKT1 Community, Rev Collective
- **Newsletters**: MKT1 Newsletter, Demand Gen Report, Exit Five, Elena Verna's Substack
- **Podcasts**: Exit Five, Revenue Vitals, Demand Gen Chat, B2B Growth
- **Conferences**: SaaStr, Pavilion GTM Summit, Marketing AI Conference
- **LinkedIn**: daily user. Follows other VPs of Marketing, posts 1-2x/week herself

### Voice (how to write to her)
- Words she uses: "pipeline", "attribution", "CAC", "ICP", "content-led", "demand gen", "North Star"
- Words she avoids: "awareness" as a primary goal, "brand building" without a pipeline connection, "viral"
- Tone: direct, no-fluff, peer-to-peer
- Length tolerance: skims. Hook must land in the first two sentences. Will read long-form if the opening earns it.

### Anti-persona (NOT this person)
- Pre-Series A founders doing their own marketing (can't afford us, wrong stage)
- Enterprise CMOs with 20-person teams (don't need us, have the internal capability)
- Marketing coordinators or individual contributors (not the decision-maker)
- Companies in consumer / B2C (different motion entirely)

### Evidence
- Source 1: 18 client intake interviews (Q4 2025 - Q1 2026)
- Source 2: Win/loss analysis from 22 closed deals (last 12 months)
- Source 3: Churn analysis - 3 clients who left early (all pre-Series B, wrong fit)
- Confidence: high

## Secondary persona: "Marcus, CEO of a B2B SaaS company"

### Quick profile
- Role: CEO/Co-founder
- Stage: Series B, $20M-$60M ARR
- Pain: he is the de-facto CMO and knows it's not sustainable. He's ready to delegate marketing but nervous about handing it to someone who won't own results.
- Goal: hire or engage someone who gives him confidence that marketing will be handled without his involvement
- How he buys: usually introduces us to Priya (the VP he's about to hire or just hired), but sometimes engages us before the VP hire to build the foundation
- Voice: results-first, impatient with process, wants to see it working before going deep on methodology

### When to write to Marcus vs Priya
- Marcus: case studies, ROI-focused content, short-form LinkedIn (he doesn't read long pieces), executive briefings
- Priya: tactical content, frameworks, "how we do X" posts, detailed playbooks
