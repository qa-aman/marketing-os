## Q1 2026 content program retro (15-04-2026)

**Result**: mixed. Blog sessions up 15% MoM by March. Pipeline impact unclear - attribution model not yet set up properly.

**Reusable insights**:
- "How we do X for clients" case-format posts generated 4x more demo requests than generic thought leadership. Applies to: all content planning - prioritize case-based and process-based content over opinion pieces.
- CEO LinkedIn posts generate 6x more impressions than company page posts on the same topic. Applies to: always ghostwrite for the CEO first, then adapt for company page.
- Newsletter subscribers who came from organic search had 2.3x higher email open rates than those from LinkedIn. Applies to: prioritize SEO to grow the list, not paid or social.
- Posts with a specific number in the headline ("67 days to first pipeline") outperformed vague benefit headlines ("get pipeline faster") by 3x on clicks. Applies to: all headline writing.

**Stop doing**:
- Publishing without a distribution plan. Two posts got under 200 impressions because they went out with no promotion beyond the company page.
- Generic "5 tips for demand gen" content. Zero demo requests from this format in Q1.
- Monthly newsletter cadence - open rates dropped to 31% in February. Moved to biweekly and rates recovered to 44%.

**Test next time**:
- CEO video clip on LinkedIn (30-60 seconds, talking head) vs written post on the same topic. Hypothesis: video gets wider reach, written gets higher-quality engagement.
- Gated vs ungated long-form content. Currently all ungated. Hypothesis: a high-quality gated report might generate fewer but more qualified leads.

## Webinar: "Why your ICP is a job title, not a situation" (28-02-2026)

**Result**: 210 registrants, 94 attended (45% show rate), 6 demo requests post-event.

**Reusable insights**:
- Outcome-based webinar titles ("Why your ICP is a job title, not a situation") outperformed feature-based titles in our January test ("ICP definition workshop"). Registrations were 2.4x higher. Applies to: all webinar titling.
- Email reminders 24 hours and 1 hour before the event drove the highest attendance. Day-of-week reminder (7 days out) had no measurable impact on show rate. Applies to: all webinar reminder sequences.
- The 15-minute Q&A generated 4 of the 6 post-event demo requests. Longer Q&A = more relationship-building. Applies to: always allocate 20+ minutes for Q&A.

**Stop doing**:
- Running webinars without a post-event nurture sequence. The week-after follow-up is where most conversions happen and we missed it for this one.

**Test next time**:
- 30-minute webinar vs 45-minute. Hypothesis: shorter format, higher attendance rate, lower Q&A time (tradeoff).
