# ICP and Personas (DD-MM-YYYY)

## Primary persona: "Maya, Head of Growth at Series B PLG SaaS"

### Demographics
- Role: Head of Growth (or VP Growth)
- Seniority: VP / Director
- Team size: 4-10 (mix of growth marketers, lifecycle, growth engineers, analysts)
- Reports to: CEO or CMO

### Firmographics
- Company stage: Series A, Series B, Series C
- Employees: 50-500
- Revenue: $5M-$50M ARR
- Industry: B2B SaaS with PLG motion (collaboration, dev tools, fintech, ops)
- Geographies: US, Canada, UK, Western Europe

### Goals (what they're measured on)
1. Self-serve ARR as % of new ARR
2. Trial-to-paid conversion rate
3. Day-7 or day-14 activation rate
4. Net dollar retention via expansion-led activation

### Pains (today)
1. Activation rate is below 50%, doesn't know which step is the bottleneck
2. Onboarding requires engineering time they don't have priority for
3. Email-only nurture isn't moving the needle on activation
4. Cannot define or measure "activated" consistently across cohorts
5. Each new feature launch makes the onboarding flow longer, not better

### Jobs-to-be-done
- "When my activation rate flatlines, I want to ship onboarding experiments without engineering, so I can find the unlock fast."
- "When I onboard a new cohort, I want to see who is stuck and where, so I can nudge them before they churn."
- "When a competitor launches a new flow, I want to react in days, not quarters."

### How they buy
- **Discovery**: peer recommendations, growth Slack communities, podcast guests, LinkedIn posts from other Heads of Growth
- **Influencers**: Lenny Rachitsky, Elena Verna, Reforge, MKT1, OpenView's PLG content
- **Decision criteria** (ranked):
  1. Time to first shipped flow (under 1 week)
  2. No engineering required
  3. Activation analytics depth
  4. Pricing scales with usage, not seats
  5. Brand and references (uses similar companies?)
- **Buying committee**: usually Maya signs alone for under $30K. For Scale tier, looped in: VP Eng, CEO, sometimes Finance.
- **Sales cycle**: 21-35 days
- **Common objections**: "we'll build it ourselves", "what's wrong with Pendo/Appcues", "yet another tool in the stack"

### Where they hang out
- **Communities**: MKT1 Slack, Reforge Slack, Lenny's Slack, Demand Curve community
- **Newsletters**: Lenny's Newsletter, MKT1 Newsletter, Reforge, OpenView, Substack growth pubs
- **Podcasts**: Lenny's Podcast, Acquired, MKT1, Pod of the Possible
- **Conferences**: SaaStr Annual, Pavilion CRO Summit, Reforge Summit
- **LinkedIn**: heavy users. Follow other Heads of Growth, comment on posts, write monthly themselves.

### Voice (how to write to them)
- Words they use: "activation", "PLG", "self-serve", "north star", "ship", "experiment", "cohort", "TTV"
- Words they avoid: "synergy", "leverage", "unlock", "best-in-class"
- Tone: peer-to-peer, dry humor, opinionated, specific
- Length tolerance: scan first, deep-dive if hooked. Always lead with the headline insight.

### Anti-persona (NOT this person)
- Solopreneurs and pre-seed founders (no budget, no scale)
- Enterprise sales-led SaaS with no self-serve motion
- B2C product managers
- Marketing operations leaders (they buy MAP/CDP, not activation tools)

### Evidence
- Source 1: 14 customer interviews (Q1 2026)
- Source 2: Closed-won analysis from 47 deals last 6 months
- Source 3: Lost-deal analysis (top reason: "we'll build it ourselves" 38%, "stuck on Pendo" 22%, "no budget" 18%)
- Confidence: high

## Secondary persona: "Dev, Senior PM running activation"

### Quick profile

- Role: Senior PM, Lead PM, Group PM owning growth or activation surface
- Company stage: same as Maya
- Goal: improve activation funnel, ship experiments
- Pain: depends on engineering for any onboarding change
- How they buy: usually the user/champion, not the buyer. Maya signs.
- Voice: technical, data-fluent, less marketing language

### When to write to Dev vs Maya

- Maya: brand, positioning, ROI content, executive-targeted ads
- Dev: technical content, integration guides, API docs, in-product comms
