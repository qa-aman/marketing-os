# How Ramp cut new-team onboarding from 11 days to 3 with Threadline

## The customer

Ramp is the corporate card and spend management platform serving 25,000+ businesses. Their self-serve motion onboards finance teams from sub-50 to 5,000-person companies. Sarah Chen, Head of Growth at Ramp, owns activation across this entire range.

## The challenge

Ramp's median time-to-first-card-issued sat at 11 days. For finance teams piloting Ramp against incumbents like Brex and Mercury, those 11 days were the most fragile window in the funnel. 34% of trial accounts went dormant before issuing a card.

Sarah's team had built three internal onboarding flows over 18 months, each requiring 2-3 weeks of engineering. Iteration was slow. Variations couldn't be tested in parallel. The growth team's roadmap was bottlenecked by engineering availability.

The cost of inaction was clear: every percentage point of activation lift was worth roughly $4M in incremental ARR. Onboarding was the single highest-leverage surface in the company, and the team that owned it could barely move it.

## The solution

Ramp evaluated Pendo, Appcues, and Threadline. Threadline won on three criteria: time to first shipped flow (Ramp's first flow shipped 4 days post-purchase), depth of activation analytics (cohort comparison out of the box), and multi-channel nudges (Slack and email, not just in-app).

> "We didn't need another product analytics tool. We needed something that could cut activation time without us hiring more engineers. Threadline was built for that exact problem." -- Sarah Chen, Head of Growth, Ramp

Implementation took one week. Sarah's team migrated their three legacy flows in parallel, using Threadline's milestone tracking to define "activated" as "first card issued and first transaction posted within 7 days."

## The results

**Median time to first card issued: 11 days -> 3 days (73% reduction).**

Within 60 days of going live:
- Day-7 activation rate increased from 48% to 67%
- Trial-to-paid conversion increased from 22% to 29%
- Engineering time spent on onboarding dropped 80% (from ~6 hours/week to under 1)
- Number of onboarding experiments shipped per month: 1 (legacy) to 8 (with Threadline)

> "We've shipped 32 onboarding experiments in 4 months. With our previous setup, we'd have shipped 4." -- Sarah Chen

## What's next

Ramp is now using Threadline to expand into vertical-specific onboarding flows: a tailored experience for SaaS finance teams, another for e-commerce ops. Each flow targets a specific persona's first 7 days, with analytics that connect activation directly to expansion revenue.
