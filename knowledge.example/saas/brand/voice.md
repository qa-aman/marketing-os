# Brand voice

## Voice in one sentence

We sound like a senior growth operator talking to another senior growth operator over a coffee: specific, fast, opinionated, and short on filler.

## 5 voice principles

1. **Specific over vague.** Numbers, named companies, and concrete examples. "Cut activation time 4x" beats "dramatically improved activation."
2. **Operator's perspective.** We are practitioners writing for practitioners. Reference real workflows, real tools, real tradeoffs.
3. **One idea per paragraph.** Two-to-three sentences max. White space is a feature.
4. **Opinionated.** We take positions. "Most onboarding flows are too long" not "There are different schools of thought on onboarding length."
5. **Plain English.** If a smart engineer outside our space would not understand a sentence on first read, rewrite it.

## Phrases we use

- "ship", "shipping", "shipped"
- "activation", "time to value", "north star metric"
- "PLG" (when audience is technical), "self-serve" (when broader)
- "in our experience", "we've seen", "the pattern is"
- "the tradeoff is", "the catch is"

## Phrases we avoid

- "leverage" (use "use")
- "unlock" (use specific action)
- "robust", "seamless", "powerful", "best-in-class", "industry-leading"
- "in today's fast-paced world", "in the digital age"
- "revolutionary", "game-changing", "transformative"
- "reach out", "circle back", "touch base"
- "exciting" (the work should speak for itself)

## Sentence length pattern

Mixed. Most sentences 8-15 words. One short sentence (3-6 words) every 3-4 sentences for rhythm. Avoid sentences over 25 words.

## Point of view

- **Blog and long-form**: first person plural ("we"). Founder posts: first person singular ("I").
- **Product copy**: second person ("you").
- **Newsletters**: first person singular if a named author, otherwise first person plural.
- **Press releases**: third person.

## Tone calibration

- **Formal scale**: 4 out of 10 (conversational, professional, but not corporate)
- **Humor**: dry, occasional. Never punny. Never quippy.
- **Confidence**: high but earned. We back claims with evidence.

## Specific style rules

- **No em dashes.** Use commas or hyphens instead.
- **No emojis** in any external content. Internal Slack OK.
- **Title case for H1s, sentence case for H2/H3.**
- **Numbers**: spell under 10 in prose, figures for 10+. Always figures for stats and metrics.
- **Dates**: DD-MM-YYYY in internal docs, "April 25, 2026" in external content.
- **Oxford comma**: yes.
- **Contractions**: yes (we're, don't, it's). Sounds human.

## Examples in voice

**Good**:
> Most onboarding flows are too long. We pulled data from 200 B2B SaaS trials last month: every additional step past 5 dropped completion 8%. The fix isn't "make it prettier." It's "delete steps."

**Bad** (don't do this):
> In today's competitive SaaS landscape, optimizing your customer onboarding journey is more critical than ever. Best-in-class teams are leveraging powerful tools to seamlessly unlock activation potential and drive transformative business outcomes.
