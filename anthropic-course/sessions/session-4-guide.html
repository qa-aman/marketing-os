From your folder to your whole stack

So far every task started the same way: Cowork read a file from verdal-marketing/ and wrote a file back into it. That covers a lot, but your real marketing work does not live only in one folder. It lives in your inbox, your team's Drive, your calendar, maybe Teams or SharePoint. Connectors close that gap.

What a connector actually does

A connector gives Cowork live access to an external tool, worked exactly the same way as a local file. Nothing about the prompt changes. You still ask Cowork to "read," "find," or "pull from" something, you just point it at a live tool instead of a document in your folder.

Everything today happens in one place: Customize > Connectors. That menu is where you turn tools on, grant access, and turn them off again later.

STEP 01

Connect a tool

Turn on one connector and sign in. This is the only setup step, and it takes a couple of minutes the first time.

Do this

  1. Open Claude Desktop and go to Customize > Connectors.
  2. Under Google Workspace, turn on Gmail or Drive (pick whichever you actually use). If your team runs on Microsoft, use Microsoft 365 instead: Outlook, OneDrive, SharePoint, and Teams each turn on separately.
  3. Sign in when prompted. A dialog will ask what Cowork can access.
  4. Grant read access. That is enough for everything in this session.
Google Workspace. Three connectors: Drive (read documents), Gmail (search threads and emails), Calendar (check events). Cowork reads a Drive document as if it were sitting in your local folder, no download needed.
Microsoft 365. Four connectors: Outlook, OneDrive, SharePoint, Teams. Each turns on separately in the same menu. Files in OneDrive and SharePoint work like local files once connected, and Teams threads become readable context.

First-time sign-in can take two to three minutes and needs browser popups allowed. If the sign-in flow stalls, check that your browser is not blocking the popup.

STEP 02

Other connectors

Google and Microsoft cover most of the room, but the connector library goes further.

Browse the full list in Customize > Connectors. Beyond Google Workspace and Microsoft 365, you will find:

  • Web scraping, to pull competitor posts or pricing pages.
  • Analytics, to pull live traffic and campaign numbers.
  • Deck builders, to push finished slides straight into a presentation tool.
Verdal example. Scrape The Sill and Bloomscape's product pages for a quick competitor snapshot, no manual copy-paste needed.

The library grows over time, so it is worth a scroll now and then. You do not need to turn any of these on today, just know where to find them.

STEP 03

Pull real data into a deliverable

The headline moment. Cowork reads a live inbox and a local file in the same task.

Paste into Cowork
the live data task 
Read my Gmail, find the latest plant-care trends email
a teammate sent, pull the key points, and add a short
"this month" section to projects/verdal-newsletter.md.

What good looks like

  • Cowork searches Gmail first, and you can watch that step in the to-do list before it touches any file.
  • It finds the right thread and pulls the actual key points, not a generic summary.
  • It opens projects/verdal-newsletter.md and adds a new "this month" section without rewriting the rest of the file.
  • The new section matches Verdal's brand voice from context/, and cites where the point came from.

Test it

Open verdal-newsletter.md after it runs. If the new section reads generic or does not reference the email, check that Gmail is connected and try again with: "search Gmail specifically, do not guess at trends."

Say this out loud while it runs: "this prompt just touched a live inbox and a local file in the same task. That is what a connector unlocks."

STEP 04

Claude in Chrome

Different from a connector: this gives Cowork an actual browser.

Claude in Chrome lets Cowork click, read, and capture what it finds on a live website. Use it when the thing you need is not stored anywhere as a document, for example checking how a brand shows up in search results, reading a competitor's current product page, or grabbing live pricing.

Paste into Cowork
a Chrome browsing task 
Check if Verdal shows up when people search for AI for
plant food. Screenshot the top three results and save
them to projects/search-audit.md.

What good looks like

  • Cowork actually visits a search results page rather than guessing from memory.
  • The screenshots or summary land in projects/search-audit.md, not just in the chat panel.
STEP 05

Safety and access

Sixty seconds that matter before you connect anything for your own brand.

The rule. Grant the minimum access a tool asks for. Read-only is usually enough for marketing work: Cowork needs to read your Gmail or Drive, not send emails or edit documents on your behalf. You can revoke any connector at any time in Customize > Connectors, and Cowork asks before doing anything outside your folder or beyond what you granted.

If a permission dialog offers a narrower option than the default, take it. You can always widen access later if a task actually needs it.
