# Session 1 slides - Meet Claude Cowork

Detailed slide spec. Each slide has its on-screen content and speaker notes. This is the source of truth: the HTML deck (`session-1.html`) is generated from it, and you can also build a PPT from it. One idea per slide.

Palette: botanical green `#2F6B4F`, cream `#F7F4EC`, terracotta `#C56B47`, ink `#1F2A24`.

---

## Slide 1 - Title
**On screen:**
- Meet Claude Cowork
- Session 1 of 6 - Cowork for the team
- From chat box to working teammate

**Notes:** Welcome them. This is the first of six short sessions. Today is about seeing why Cowork is a different tool from the chat they already know.

## Slide 2 - Today's promise
**On screen:**
- By the end of this session you will have handed Cowork a real task and watched it produce a finished file on your own laptop.
- One rule: watch first, then you try.

**Notes:** Set expectations. Nobody types until the lab. Watch the demo, then do it yourself.

## Slide 3 - Today's agenda
**On screen (4 items):**
1. The task loop - how Cowork plans and works
2. Setup and permissions - what it can and cannot touch
3. Files - real docs, decks, sheets, images
4. The model picker - the right model per task

**Notes:** Four ideas today. Small on purpose. We go deep, not wide.

## Slide 4 - The problem
**On screen:**
- Most people install Cowork and keep using it like a chat box.
- They are using a fraction of it.

**Notes:** This is the hook. Ask: who here has used Claude Chat? Good. Cowork looks similar but works completely differently.

## Slide 5 - Chat vs Cowork
**On screen (two columns):**
- Claude Chat: runs in the cloud, hands you text, you copy, paste, and save it yourself.
- Claude Cowork: a local desktop agent, reads your folder, does the work, saves the finished file back.

**Notes:** The key difference: Chat gives you words, Cowork gives you finished files in your own folder. For most marketing work, Cowork is where the work actually happens.

## Slide 6 - The one-line idea
**On screen (big quote):**
- "Chat is a smart colleague on a phone call. Cowork is that colleague sitting at your computer, with your files open, doing the work."

**Notes:** Let this land. Pause. This is the mental model for the whole course.

## Slide 7 - The task loop
**On screen (3 steps):**
- 1. Makes a plan
- 2. Works step by step, showing a to-do list
- 3. Saves the finished file
- Footer: You watch and steer. It is not a black box.

**Notes:** Point out that you can see what it is doing and stop or redirect it any time. This is what makes longer tasks trustworthy.

## Slide 8 - Setup and permissions
**On screen:**
- Cowork only touches the folder you point it at.
- It asks permission the first time.
- You stay in control of what it can see.

**Notes:** Reassure the non-technical room. It cannot wander your whole computer. You grant access to one folder.

## Slide 9 - Files and the model picker
**On screen (two points):**
- Files: Cowork reads and writes real docs, decks, sheets, and images. Output lands in your folder, not a chat window.
- Model picker: choose a heavier model for hard thinking, a faster one for simple jobs.

**Notes:** Show where the model picker sits in the app during the demo. Most of the time the default is fine.

## Slide 10 - Demo (live)
**On screen:**
- Watch: Cowork builds a 12-slide Verdal strategy deck from the brand files.
- (holding slide - the live app is the star)

**Notes:** Switch to Cowork. Load Verdal, grant access, show the context files, paste the demo prompt, narrate the task loop out loud, open the finished deck. Key line: "I did not paste any brand details. It knew them because they live in the folder."

## Slide 11 - Your turn
**On screen:**
- Load your Verdal folder. Hand Cowork a first task. Watch the loop. Open the file.
- The exact steps are on your lab worksheet.
- 45 minutes. We are walking the room.

**Notes:** Attendees work on their own laptops. Circulate. Watch for the two common blockers: folder access not granted, and looking for output in the chat instead of the folder.

## Slide 12 - Debrief
**On screen (3 questions):**
- What surprised you?
- Where did it save the file?
- How was this different from Chat?

**Notes:** Let a few people answer each. This cements the mental model better than you repeating it.

## Slide 13 - Next time and homework
**On screen:**
- Next: Make Cowork yours, so it remembers your brand and your rules.
- Homework: hand Cowork one more Verdal task before next session.

**Notes:** Send them off with momentum and one small action.
