# Ideal customer profile - Verdal

## Primary persona: "Plant-proud Priya"

Priya, 30, lives in a mid-size US city apartment with 15+ houseplants. Treats them like pets and names them. Combined household income $85k+. Reads care guides, follows plant accounts on Instagram, and asks the plant shop staff by name.

**What she wants:** healthy, thriving plants without a horticulture degree. Products she can trust, explained plainly.

**What she fears:** wasting money on hype, killing a plant she loves, being the person whose plants always die.

**Where she is:** Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok, email, the occasional Reddit deep-dive (r/houseplants) before a bigger purchase.

**What makes her buy:** a specific nutrient or care story, a real review from someone like her, a horticulturist's name attached, free shipping over a threshold.

**What makes her churn:** her plants don't visibly improve, the subscription feels like a trap, the brand starts shouting.

## Secondary persona: "New-plant Nate"

Nate, 27, just bought his first few plants. Overwhelmed by choice and a little anxious about killing them. High intent, low knowledge. Wants a simple routine someone trustworthy picked for him.

**Best hook for Nate:** "Here's the simple weekly routine for your first plants." Bundles and starter guides convert him.

## Who we are NOT for

- Bargain hunters who sort by lowest price
- Serious horticulturists who want raw hydroponic chemistry
- People who see plants as disposable decor

## Buying triggers by stage (use for content and ads)

| Awareness stage | What Priya is thinking | What to give her |
|---|---|---|
| Unaware | "My plant looks fine" | Educational: signs you might miss |
| Problem-aware | "Why are the leaves yellow?" | The cause, plainly explained |
| Solution-aware | "Does plant food actually help?" | Nutrient story + proof |
| Product-aware | "Is Verdal better than The Sill?" | Honest comparison, reviews |
| Most aware | "I'm ready, is there a deal?" | Care Box offer, free shipping |
