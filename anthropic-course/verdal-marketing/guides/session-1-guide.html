Claude Cowork for marketers - Session 1 of 6

Meet Claude Cowork: hand it your first real task

Cowork is a local desktop agent. You give it a folder, it reads your files, does multi-step work, and saves finished files back. By the end of this session you will load a real brand folder, hand Cowork a task, and watch it save a finished file. This handout is your step by step: a little theory, then the exact prompts to paste.

Worked brand: Verdal, a plant care brand. Dataset: verdal-marketing/. Official course: Introduction to Claude Cowork.

What you need before you start. Claude Desktop installed and signed in, with Cowork in the sidebar. The verdal-marketing folder copied onto your computer. If you do not have Cowork yet, install it first, then come back. A shared login works for watching, but for the hands-on parts you want your own login.
The one idea for the whole session. Claude Chat hands you text that you copy out yourself. Claude Cowork hands you finished files, saved into your own folder. That difference is the point.

What you will do

How Cowork works

Two things make Cowork different from the chat box, and both matter for the rest of the course.

Chat vs Cowork

Claude ChatClaude Cowork
Where it runsIn the cloudOn your computer, in a folder you choose
What you getText to copy and pasteFinished files, saved into your folder
What it can readOnly what you paste inEvery file in the folder you give it
Best forQuick thinking and draftingGetting the actual work done

The task loop

When you hand Cowork a task, it does not answer in one shot. It works in a loop you can watch:

  1. Makes a plan and shows it as a to-do list.
  2. Works step by step, ticking items off as it goes.
  3. Saves the finished file into your folder.

You can watch the to-do list and stop or redirect it at any point. That is what makes it safe to give it longer jobs.

STEP 01

Set up and load your folder

Point Cowork at the Verdal folder. This is the workspace for every demo and lab today.

Do this

  1. Open Claude Desktop and click Cowork in the sidebar.
  2. Load the verdal-marketing folder.
  3. When it asks for permission, click Allow. This lets Cowork read and edit files in that one folder, and nowhere else.

Here is what is inside the folder, so you know what Cowork can see:

verdal-marketing/ 
context/                 who Verdal is - Cowork reads this first
  brand-context.md
  brand-voice.md
  ideal-customer-profile.md
templates/
  strategy-deck-outline.md
  carousel-design-system.md
data/                    real numbers for later sessions
brand-photos/            images for the strategy deck
projects/                your finished work is saved here
Why this matters. Everything Cowork makes today will sound like Verdal because the brand lives in context/. You never paste brand rules into the chat. Cowork reads them from the folder every time.
STEP 02

The task loop and permissions

Before you hand over a real task, know what you are watching and what Cowork can touch.

What you will see

As Cowork works, a to-do list appears. For the strategy deck coming up, it looks like this:

cowork to-do list 
[x] Read brand context
[x] Research plant-care trends
[>] Build the 12-slide outline
[ ] Generate slides
[ ] Save to projects/
You are in control. Cowork only touches the folder you pointed it at, and it asked permission the first time. If a task ever tries to do something you did not expect, you can stop it. Start cautious, and review what it changed before you rely on it.
STEP 03

Your first task

A small, safe task so you can watch the loop end to end and find the saved file.

Paste into Cowork
your first task 
Read the brand context in context/ and write three
on-brand Instagram captions about repotting a plant.
Save them to projects/repotting-captions.md

Pick one, so everyone's output is different:

  • Three Instagram captions about repotting a plant
  • A warm welcome message for a first-time plant owner
  • A myth-buster post, "you are probably not overwatering"

What good looks like

  • Cowork reads the context files before it writes anything.
  • The captions sound warm and plain, and lead with the plant, not the product (that is Verdal's voice).
  • A new file appears at projects/repotting-captions.md. Open it.

Test it

Open the file in projects/. If the captions read like generic AI, the brand voice file was not read. Try again and add: "follow context/brand-voice.md exactly."

STEP 04

The headline build: a strategy deck

The big one. Cowork reads the brand, follows a template, uses real photos, and saves a finished deck. This is the demo that shows what Cowork can really do.

Paste into Cowork
the strategy deck 
Load my verdal-marketing folder. Read the brand context
in context/, then build a 12-slide on-brand marketing
strategy deck using templates/strategy-deck-outline.md
and the images in brand-photos/. Save it to projects/.

What good looks like

  • It follows the 12-slide template, one idea per slide.
  • The strategy is tailored to Verdal, with real positioning, not generic advice.
  • It uses the brand photos, and green is an accent, not a flood.
  • A .pptx lands in projects/.
The first draft is a draft. A photo may need repositioning. That is normal. The win is a strong, on-brand starting point in minutes, not a perfect final deck. Say what to fix and it will redo that slide.

Say this while it runs, out loud: "I did not paste any brand details. It knew them because they live in the folder." That is the lesson of the session.

STEP 05

The model picker

One small control worth knowing on day one.

Next to the prompt box is a model picker. You can switch which model runs a task: a heavier model for hard thinking like strategy, a faster one for simple jobs like a quick caption. Most of the time the default is fine.

Try it

Rerun your captions task with a different model and notice the difference in speed and depth. There is no wrong choice here, this is just so you know the control exists.

Check yourself

Try these a day or two after the session, answers below.

Q: What does Cowork do differently from Chat?
A: It works locally on your files in a folder and saves finished output there, not just text in a chat window.
Q: Where does Cowork save the files it makes?
A: In the folder you loaded, under projects/.
Q: What does the task loop show you while Cowork works?
A: A step by step to-do list you can watch and steer, not one big black box.
