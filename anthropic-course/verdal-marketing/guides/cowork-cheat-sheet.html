Claude Cowork for marketers

Cowork cheat sheet

The whole course on one page. Keep it at your desk.

The three golden rules.
  1. Cowork works on the folder you point it at. Finished work saves to projects/.
  2. Grant the minimum access. Review what it changed before anything ships.
  3. Check facts against your source files. Never publish a claim you cannot trace to a file.

What Cowork can do

CapabilityWhat it isExample prompt
The task loop Cowork plans, works step by step, then saves a finished file. Read context/ and write 3 Instagram captions about repotting. Save to projects/captions.md
Standing context Global instructions Cowork remembers across every task, so you stop repeating your rules. Always follow context/brand-voice.md and never use em dashes in anything you write for Verdal
Projects The folder Cowork reads from and saves finished work into. Load my verdal-marketing folder and build a 12-slide strategy deck, save it to projects/
Skills A reusable workflow you package once and run again with one line. Package this weekly brief workflow into a reusable skill
Plugins Bundles of skills and tools you install to extend what Cowork can do. Install a plugin that lets me export decks straight to Google Slides
Connectors Links to outside tools, like Slack or Google Drive, so Cowork can read or send data there. Connect Google Drive and pull the latest customer survey results into data/
Claude in Chrome Claude working inside your browser, reading pages and filling forms for you. Open our competitor's pricing page and summarise their plans into projects/competitor-pricing.md
Live artifacts Interactive pages, like a dashboard or calculator, that update live in the browser. Build a live artifact showing our email open rates by month from data/email-stats.csv
Scheduled tasks Tasks Cowork runs on a repeating schedule, without you asking each time. Every Monday at 9am, read data/ and draft the weekly plant-care newsletter to projects/
Prompting in one line

Say what you want, point at the files to use, and say where to save it.

When not to use it

  • Real quotes or attribution
  • Legal sign-off
  • Anything where a wrong fact costs money
  • Real customer data
