# Carousel examples Verdal likes

Reference layouts for the carousel design system. Used in Session 2. These describe the styles we want Cowork to learn from.

## Example 1 - "Why" explainer (our favourite)
- Slide 1: a plain question hook. "Why are the leaves yellow?"
- Slide 2: the one-sentence reason.
- Slides 3 to 5: one fix per slide, short.
- Slide 6: a soft close and next step.
- Look: cream background, green headline, one terracotta accent per slide, lots of space.

## Example 2 - Myth vs truth
- Slide 1: "You have been told to water weekly. Here is the truth."
- Slides 2 to 6: myth on top, truth below, one per slide.
- Look: split layout, green and cream, small plant illustration corner.

## Example 3 - Seasonal checklist
- Slide 1: "Your spring plant care checklist."
- Slides 2 to 6: one task per slide with a checkbox feel.
- Look: warm, friendly, terracotta checkmarks.

## Rules for every carousel
- One idea per slide. If it needs two, it is two slides.
- Headline in sentence case, large, top third.
- Lead with the plant, not the product.
- Brand green as the anchor, terracotta as the accent, cream background.
- No hype, no shame, no emoji walls. Follow context/brand-voice.md.
